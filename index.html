Dr. Aura Obreja

Postdoctoral researcher in computational astrophysics
@Astro-AI Lab

I’m a curious person with a passion for physics. During my undergraduate studies I discover that all physics is exciting, with the notable exception of metrology. This motivated me to pursue postgraduate studies in astrophysics because astrophysics incorporates everything, from the quantum field theory relevant to the Big Bang and inflation epoch, to the nuclear physics and general relativity of evolving stars and black holes. The universe is a giant one event only laboratory, at least from outside of the multiverse perspective. Simulations and solving all sorts of inverse problems is my way of getting closer to understand it.

Learn about what I do

My working tools

My research focused on cosmological simulations of galaxies starting from the initial conditions of the early universe and following their evolution to the present day. Simulating galaxies is incredibly demanding in terms of computational costs because the dynamical range of the physical processes involved spans many orders of magnitude, and these processes are all connected together to various degrees. These limitations force us to cut corners when designing simulation codes, sometimes even corners that are probably important. My aim in astrophysics is to disentangle this complexity by taking fewer shortcuts. In particular, I want to simulate how radiation modifies the astrophysical gas dynamics on the scale of galaxies and beyond with reasonable computational resources. In this respect, the advances in machine learning and AI provide the powerful tools needed to make this leap in galaxy formation. AI also nicely brings my lifelong interest in science fiction full circle.

Research Projects

Few highlights from my research endeavor.

Galactic Structure Finder (gsf)

A Python tool to identify dynamical structures in galaxies

Stellar and Bremstrahlung radiation effects in simulated galaxies on-the-fly

Developed radiative transfer for cosmological simulations

GRASIL-3D

A radiative transfer, ray tracing code which couples dust models with cosmological simulations

AGN radiation effects in simulated galaxies

Constrained predictions from a post-processed cosmological simulation and comparison to state-of-the-art observational datasets

The QSO MUSEUM survey

The largest statistical survey of quasar's circumgalctic medium in emission

Estimating CGM and AGN properties with simulations and machine learning

From a large database of photoionisation models to firm constraints
